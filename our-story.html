Our Story

Our Journey: A Tale of Destiny and Love

In the fateful year of 2017, destiny had a plan for us, and the NYSC (National Youth Service Corps) camp became the stage where our love story unfolded. The campgrounds provided the backdrop for our potential, and it was there that Ihuoma and I first crossed paths. We were both actively engaged in the three-week camp hosted by Anambra State NYSC, and it was an experience that would shape our future in ways we couldn't have imagined.

We found ourselves in the same squad nominated for the swearing-in ceremony parade, and as fate would have it, I had the honor of leading that squad. Shortly after, I was promoted to the role of General Parade Commander, making me the No. 1 Corps member. Meanwhile, Ihuoma joined the parade band, showcasing her own unique talents and charm.

It was clear to both of us that there was a powerful chemistry between us, but we couldn't predict what lay ahead once the camp ended. Ihuoma was a remarkable woman - cool, classy, and spontaneous. I never thought I was on her level, and I was hesitant to approach her. She was not only beautiful but also intelligent, cheerful, sweet, and always had a charming smile on her face. Although I managed to collect her phone number during our time at camp, I was too nervous to call her. Regrettably, I lost her contact when my phone was stolen.

Our paths crossed once again at a town hall meeting hosted by the Governor of the State, and this time, I made sure to collect her number and safeguard it, determined not to lose it again. Our service year eventually came to an end, and we went our separate ways, unsure if we'd ever meet again.

A year and some months later, I couldn't get her out of my mind. Knowing that I was quite recognizable due to my role as the parade commander, I decided to reach out. When she heard from me, Ihuoma was delighted, and our conversations rew. I always made it clear that I was genuinely interested in her, even though I still felt nervous because of the way she carried herself.

On November 25, 2022, she noticed a WhatsApp status of mine that read, "Even if her profile says TAKEN, still DM her, she might be TAKEN for GRANTED." Her response was a simple "lol," and that opened the door for me to engage further. I entered her DM with a mixture of care, a sprinkle of love, inquiries about her, and a shoulder to cry on.

Our journey began, despite the distance that separated us - Umuahia and Abuja. On January 23rd, we officially started dating, and on July 18th, I proposed. Then, on October 7th, we celebrated our love with a traditional marriage. Our families exchanged gifts, shared food, and contacts, while our parents blessed our union with their prayers.

As we invite you to celebrate with us on December 10th, we humbly request your prayers and blessings for our special day. Your presence and beautiful gifts will make our wedding even more memorable. We can't wait to see you there!
